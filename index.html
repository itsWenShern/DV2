Deforestation in Malaysia

A Decade of Change, Impact, and Conservation Efforts

How Much Forest Has Been Lost, and Where?

In 2024, tree cover loss in Malaysia was overwhelmingly dominated by East Malaysia. Sarawak (≈ 80,000 hectares) and Sabah (≈ 75,000 hectares) recorded the highest losses by a significant margin.

Their combined deforestation vastly exceeds all other states. A sharp drop to third-place Pahang (≈ 39,000 ha) underscores this clear geographic concentration of loss.

From 2014-2024, all five states showed a general decline in annual tree cover loss. Sarawak consistently reported the highest deforestation, peaking in 2014 and trending significantly downwards since.

Sabah and Pahang followed, showing similar decreasing trends. Johor and Perak recorded significantly lower and more stable losses throughout the period.
